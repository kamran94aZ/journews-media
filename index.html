Technology 24.05.2026

Future of Technology

Kamran Muradov's ArticleToday, I will comment on a recent report by the BBC. According to the BBC's investigation, we will witness significant technological changes over the next 24 years. The report discusses the rapid development of quantum computers, substantial breakthroughs in nanotechnologies, technological revolutions in the medical field, traffic-free roads, and lunar travel models. It states that the events depicted in Tom Cruise's movie will cease to be mere science fiction. You can obtain detailed information about this from the article in the link.
However, according to my commentary, the spread of these technological revolutions across the world and the establishment of a single global technocracy do not reflect reality for the current era, especially at a time when medium and small-scale wars persist in the Middle East region, monarchical regimes still rule in certain countries, and scandals like the Epstein scandal continue to unfold. In fact, a period of stagnation is currently being experienced in the field of technological revolutions. In my opinion, AI itself was the breakthrough. In the next 26 years, investments will merely be directed toward the development of existing technologies.
In other words, during an era dominated by economic recessions, we are left with two options. I believe that the economic power of states, including the United States, will be deliberately weakened to pave the way for technocratic leaders like Elon Musk. Economic crises prove this to us. Nanotechnology, quantum computers, medical technological achievements, and lunar colonization issues will continue at the same pace and with a slow flow.
In particular, a greater revolutionary leap in the field of artificial intelligence might only be possible toward the late 2030s with the development of microchip technology; however, even this will not be a complete revolutionary leap, but will simply follow the development paradigm of existing technologies. In short, my expectation is that in 2050, there will not be a second revolutionary turnaround that will completely surprise us, but rather predictable changes within the scope of our current expectations.
